Milton Antonio Jurado Alvarado

Milton Antonio Jurado Alvarado was born in Quito Ecuador in 1953. He first came to California in 1972 where along with other Latino artist he helped start Self-Help Graphics in East Los Angeles. He joined other Mexican and Chicano artist such us Sister Karen Bocalero, Carlos Bueno and Antonio Ibanez to explore and promote a new king of artist expression which was yet unknown by the local artist world. Until this group came together the Hispanic artist scene was fragmented figures launched and had yet to develop his own identity. Working out of an abandoned theater these pioneering figures lauched a new kind of art presentations. With a grant from local sponsors these artist created a mobil art studio built on a truck that travelled throughout the community teaching art to children of all ages. On a more personal level each of the founding members relied on their own backgrounds to determine how their self-expression was best defined. Due to his limited artistic experience in Ecuador Maja Brough with him the knowledge of an art form that was not yet known to the artistic sculptures made from the unlikely medium of everyday bread. Inexpensive and available everyday, Maja was able to create miniature sculptures of human figures out of this most unexpected medium. Not surprisingly, these small creations found an audience in the local art scene. Not finding a really expansive audience for his Eucadorian inspired pieces he moved to the world of furniture. For more than thirty years Maja designed, hand painted and manufactured some of the most captivating wrought-iron home furnishing that were a hit from cost to coast. In New York he showed his eye dazzling pieces at the International Contemporary Furniture Show at the Jacob Javits Convention Center. For more than 5 years his tables, chairs and painted jewelry boxes kept him commercially success. Maja was the first artist to open a retail art store in the Sunset Junction-Silverlake area. There he develop a reputation for some of the most interesting functional art on the scene at the time. It was at this juncture in his career that he turned to a more formalized genre of art. In particular he was inspired by the world of painting where his interest in landscape and social justice matter captured his attention. Like this predecessors in Mexico including Rivera, Siqueriors and Orozco this South American immigrant gravitated to the planet of the undocumented and the indigenous people of the world.
